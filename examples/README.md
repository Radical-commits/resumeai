## Tips for Writing Your Resume

### Personal Information
- Use your real contact information
- Ensure LinkedIn and GitHub URLs are complete
- Keep phone number in international format

### Summary
- 2-3 sentences maximum
- Highlight years of experience
- Mention key expertise areas
- Focus on your unique value

### Highlights
- 3-4 bullet points
- Use measurable achievements
- Include specific numbers when possible
- Show impact, not just activities

### Skills
- Group by category (technical, tools, frameworks, etc.)
- List most relevant skills first
- Be honest about your proficiency
- Update regularly as you learn

### Experience
- List in reverse chronological order (most recent first)
- Use action verbs (Led, Built, Implemented, Designed)
- Include measurable results (%, $, numbers)
- Show progression in your career
- Focus on achievements, not responsibilities

### Education
- Include degree, institution, location, graduation date
- Add honors/awards if applicable
- List relevant coursework for recent graduates
- Include GPA only if 3.5+ and recent

### Optional Sections
Include these if relevant:
- **Certifications**: Professional credentials
- **Projects**: Side projects, open source contributions
- **Awards**: Professional recognition
- **Publications**: Articles, papers, blog posts
- **Languages**: If you speak multiple languages

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Using placeholder text (Your Name, City, Country)
❌ Including outdated or irrelevant experience
❌ Writing paragraphs instead of bullet points
❌ Focusing on duties instead of achievements
❌ Missing contact information or broken links
❌ Inconsistent date formats
❌ Typos and grammatical errors

## Best Practices

✅ Quantify achievements with numbers
✅ Use strong action verbs
✅ Tailor content to your target role
✅ Keep descriptions concise and scannable
✅ Update regularly (every 3-6 months)
✅ Proofread multiple times
✅ Get feedback from peers
